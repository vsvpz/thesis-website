This experiment began with picking out 10 objects that I would be using to create photograms (a method of photography-based image-making that was unkown to me). I decided to pick up small trinkets and jewellery - chachkis, if you will - around my apartment that were of sentimental value to me. From a ring my grandmother gave me to small ceramic frogs my aunt had sent me off with as a good luck charm, I had no idea what kind of images these objects would produce.

photogram

A photogram is a photographic print made by laying objects onto photographic paper and exposing it to light.

As defined by the Tate Modern.

Once I had made my photograms I decided to scan them to be able to keep a digital, high resolution copy. This led to messing around with dragging the photograms in different directions and at different speeds during the scanning process. By doing this I was able to produce all of the image studies shown on the right, creating textured patterns and haunting silhouettes.
